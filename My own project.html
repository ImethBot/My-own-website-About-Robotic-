What is Technology?

Technology is the use of scientific knowledge to create tools, techniques, and processes that help people solve problems, improve their lives, and achieve goals. It can include simple tools like hammers and wheels, or complex systems like computers and the internet.

Electronic technology

Electronic technology is the design, production, and maintenance of electronic devices, systems, software, and control circuits. It's used in many areas of daily life and industrial production, including:

  • Computer aided circuit design and production
  • Radio and TV technologies
  • Civil and military communication systems
  • Satellite communication systems
  • Factories that manufacture industrial electronic devices

Mechanical technology

Mechanical technology is the application of engineering principles and scientific concepts to the design, operation, and testing of machines and equipment. It involves practical skills and the creation of useful products and production machinery.

Medical technology

Medical technology is the use of scientific knowledge and tools to diagnose, treat, and improve a person's health. It can include a variety of devices and software

communications technology

Communication technology, also known as Information and Communication Technology (ICT), is a broad term for the tools and systems that allow people and organizations to interact and exchange information:

  • Devices: Computers, landlines, and mobile phones
  • Applications: Video chat, messenger, social media, and email
  • Other technologies: The internet, wireless networks, software, middleware, and live and recorded broadcasting technologies

industrial and manufacturing technologies

Industrial and manufacturing technologies are a set of technologies and methodologies that are used to improve the efficiency, quality, and productivity of industrial processes

Robotics

Line Following Robot

A line-following robot is a mobile machine that can autonomously detect and follow a line drawn on the floor. The line can be visible, like a black line on a white surface, or invisible, like a magnetic field.

Bluetooth control robot

A Bluetooth robot is a robot that uses Bluetooth technology to communicate with a remote device, such as a smartphone or computer, to allow the user to control it. Bluetooth is a wireless communication protocol that uses radio waves to exchange data over short distances.

Micro mouse

A Micromouse is a small, autonomous robot that navigates a maze to find the shortest path to the center.

Line Following Robot

Build your own robot with these simple steps!

Step 1: Gather Materials

Make sure you have all the necessary components:

  1. Microcontroller: Arduino Uno, Nano, or similar.
  2. Sensors: IR sensors or a color sensor.
  3. Motor Driver: L298N, L293D, or similar.
  4. Motors: Two DC motors (or geared motors).
  5. Chassis: Robot frame or DIY platform.
  6. Battery: 9V or Li-ion rechargeable battery.

Step 2: Build the Chassis

  1. Assemble the base using wood, acrylic, or 3D printing.
  2. Fix the two DC motors onto the chassis.
  3. Mount wheels and attach a caster wheel for stability.

Step 3: Set Up the Sensors

  1. Install two or three IR sensors at the front, pointing downward.
  2. Ensure optimal positioning for accurate line detection.

Step 4: Connect the Electronics

  1. Microcontroller: Attach securely to the chassis.
  2. Motor Driver: Wire it to motors and Arduino.
  3. Sensors: Connect VCC, GND, and signal pins.
  4. Power: Connect a battery for motors and logic circuits.

Micromouse Robot Guide

Step-by-step instructions to build a maze-navigating robot.

Step 1: Understand the Requirements

Learn the rules of the competition. The robot must autonomously navigate a maze and find the shortest path to the goal.

Step 2: Gather Components

Collect the following components:

  • Microcontroller: Arduino Nano, ESP32, or STM32
  • Two DC motors with encoders
  • Motor driver: L298N or DRV8833
  • Sensors: IR proximity sensors, Gyroscope (e.g., MPU6050)
  • Battery: LiPo (7.4V) and voltage regulator
  • Lightweight chassis, wheels, and caster wheel

Step 3: Design the Chassis

  1. Choose lightweight materials like acrylic or 3D-printed plastic.
  2. Attach motors, sensors, and battery for proper weight distribution.

Step 4: Assemble the Electronics

Connect the components:

  1. Wire the motor driver to the motors and microcontroller.
  2. Connect the IR sensors and gyroscope to the microcontroller.
  3. Attach the battery to power the system.

Step 5: Write the Code

Program the microcontroller:

  1. Control motors using PWM.
  2. Read data from sensors and gyroscope.
  3. Implement a maze-solving algorithm like Flood Fill or Wall Follower.
        // Example: Simple Wall Follower Algorithm
        if (left_sensor_detects_wall) {
            if (front_sensor_detects_wall) {
                turn_right();
            } else {
                move_forward();
            }
        } else {
            turn_left();
        }

Step 6: Test and Optimize

  1. Test each component (motors, sensors).
  2. Fine-tune movement and wall detection.
  3. Adjust speed for optimal navigation.
